Seoul, South Korea

Seoul, the capital of South Korea, is a vibrant metropolis blending modern skyscrapers with historic temples and palaces. With a population of over 9 million, it's one of Asia's most dynamic cities, known for its technology-driven economy, rich cultural heritage, and lively street markets.

Key attractions include Gyeongbokgung Palace, the bustling shopping districts of Myeongdong and Dongdaemun, and the picturesque Han River. Seoul is also famous for its food scene, offering everything from street food to high-end dining. The city's efficient public transportation system, including its extensive metro network, makes it easy to explore.

Visited places

Gyeongbokgung Palace

Gyeongbokgung Palace is one of the most iconic landmarks in Seoul and a symbol of South Korea’s rich history. Built in 1395 during the Joseon Dynasty, it served as the main royal palace and the seat of government for over 500 years. The palace's name means "Palace Greatly Blessed by Heaven."

LOTTE WORLD AQUARIUM

LOTTE WORLD AQUARIUM is one of the largest and most popular aquariums in South Korea, located within the Lotte World Mall in the Songpa District of Seoul.

Spanning over 11,000 square meters, LOTTE WORLD AQUARIUM is home to around 55,000 sea creatures across 650 species. The aquarium is divided into themed zones, each representing different aquatic environments such as the Amazon River, the ocean's deep sea, and polar regions. One of the main highlights is the 25-meter-long ocean tunnel, where visitors can walk through and observe large marine animals like sharks, rays, and sea turtles swimming overhead

Namsan Tower

Namsan Tower, also known as N-Seoul Tower, is a prominent landmark located on Namsan Mountain in the center of Seoul. The tower, which stands at 236 meters (774 feet) tall, offers panoramic views of the city and the surrounding landscape. the tower is beautifully illuminated and often features colorful light displays.

One of the most popular activities at Namsan Tower is the “Locks of Love” tradition, where visitors attach padlocks to the fences around the tower as a symbol of their love and commitment. The tower is accessible by a cable car, a scenic hike, or by bus, making it a versatile destination for both locals and tourists.

Other Popular Destinations

  • Myeongdong: A bustling shopping district with everything from high-end boutiques to street food vendors.
  • Dongdaemun Design Plaza: A modern architectural marvel and a hub for design and culture.
  • Cheonggyecheon: offers a peaceful escape from the bustling city, with pathways for walking, cycling, and relaxing.
